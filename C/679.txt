       <h3 id="changing-your-profile-picture"><a href="#changing-your-profile-picture">Changing your profile picture</a></h3> 
       <p>Your profile picture helps identify you across GitHub in pull requests, comments, contributions pages, and graphs.</p> 
       <p>When you sign up for an account, GitHub provides you with a randomly generated "identicon". <a href="https://github.com/blog/1586-identicons">Your identicon</a> generates from a hash of your user ID, so there's no way to control its color or pattern. You can replace your identicon with an image that represents you.</p> 
       <div class="extended-markdown tip border rounded-1 mb-4 p-3 color-border-info color-bg-info f5"> 
        <p><strong>Tip</strong>: Your profile picture should be a PNG, JPG, or GIF file under 1 MB in size. For the best quality rendering, we recommend keeping the image at about 500 by 500 pixels.</p> 
       </div> 
       <h4 id="setting-a-profile-picture"><a href="#setting-a-profile-picture">Setting a profile picture</a></h4> 
       <ol> 
        <li>In the upper-right corner of any page, click your profile photo, then click <strong>Settings</strong>. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/settings/userbar-account-settings.png" alt="Settings icon in the user bar"></span></li> 
        <li>Under <strong>Profile Picture</strong>, click 
         <svg version="1.1" width="16" height="16" viewbox="0 0 16 16" class="octicon octicon-pencil" aria-label="The edit icon" role="img">
          <path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M11.013 1.427a1.75 1.75 0 012.474 0l1.086 1.086a1.75 1.75 0 010 2.474l-8.61 8.61c-.21.21-.47.364-.756.445l-3.251.93a.75.75 0 01-.927-.928l.929-3.25a1.75 1.75 0 01.445-.758l8.61-8.61zm1.414 1.06a.25.25 0 00-.354 0L10.811 3.75l1.439 1.44 1.263-1.263a.25.25 0 000-.354l-1.086-1.086zM11.189 6.25L9.75 4.81l-6.286 6.287a.25.25 0 00-.064.108l-.558 1.953 1.953-.558a.249.249 0 00.108-.064l6.286-6.286z" />
         </svg> <strong>Edit</strong>. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/edit-profile-photo.png" alt="Edit profile picture"></span></li> 
        <li>Click <strong>Upload a photo...</strong>. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/edit-profile-picture-options.png" alt="Update profile picture"></span></li> 
        <li>Crop your picture. When you're done, click <strong>Set new profile picture</strong>. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/avatar_crop_and_save.png" alt="Crop uploaded photo"></span></li> 
       </ol> 
       <h4 id="resetting-your-profile-picture-to-the-identicon"><a href="#resetting-your-profile-picture-to-the-identicon">Resetting your profile picture to the identicon</a></h4> 
       <ol> 
        <li>In the upper-right corner of any page, click your profile photo, then click <strong>Settings</strong>. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/settings/userbar-account-settings.png" alt="Settings icon in the user bar"></span></li> 
        <li>Under <strong>Profile Picture</strong>, click 
         <svg version="1.1" width="16" height="16" viewbox="0 0 16 16" class="octicon octicon-pencil" aria-label="The edit icon" role="img">

If your email address is associated with a <a href="https://en.gravatar.com/">Gravatar</a>, you cannot revert to your identicon. Click <strong>Revert to Gravatar</strong> instead. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/edit-profile-picture-options.png" alt="Update profile picture"></span></li>

       <h3 id="changing-your-profile-name"><a href="#changing-your-profile-name">Changing your profile name</a></h3> 
       <p>You can change the name that is displayed on your profile. This name may also be displayed next to comments you make on private repositories owned by an organization. For more information, see "<a href="/en/articles/managing-the-display-of-member-names-in-your-organization">Managing the display of member names in your organization</a>."</p> 
       <ol> 
        <li>In the upper-right corner of any page, click your profile photo, then click <strong>Settings</strong>. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/settings/userbar-account-settings.png" alt="Settings icon in the user bar"></span></li> 
        <li>Under "Name", type the name you want to be displayed on your profile. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/name-field.png" alt="Name field in profile settings"></span></li> 
       </ol> 
       <h3 id="adding-a-bio-to-your-profile"><a href="#adding-a-bio-to-your-profile">Adding a bio to your profile</a></h3> 
       <p>Add a bio to your profile to share information about yourself with other GitHub users. With the help of <a href="/en/articles/basic-writing-and-formatting-syntax">@mentions</a> and emoji, you can include information about where you currently or have previously worked, what type of work you do, or even what kind of coffee you drink.</p> 
       <p>For a longer-form and more prominent way of displaying customized information about yourself, you can also use a profile README. For more information, see "<a href="/en/github/setting-up-and-managing-your-github-profile/managing-your-profile-readme">Managing your profile README</a>."</p> 
       <div class="extended-markdown note border rounded-1 mb-4 p-3 color-border-info color-bg-info f5"> 
        <p><strong>Note:</strong> If you have the activity overview section enabled for your profile and you @mention an organization you're a member of in your profile bio, then that organization will be featured first in your activity overview. For more information, see "<a href="/en/articles/showing-an-overview-of-your-activity-on-your-profile">Showing an overview of your activity on your profile</a>."</p> 
       </div> 
       <ol> 
        <li> <p>In the upper-right corner of any page, click your profile photo, then click <strong>Settings</strong>. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/settings/userbar-account-settings.png" alt="Settings icon in the user bar"></span></p> </li> 
        <li> <p>Under <strong>Bio</strong>, add the content that you want displayed on your profile. The bio field is limited to 160 characters. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/bio-field.png" alt="Update bio on profile"></span></p> 
         <div class="extended-markdown tip border rounded-1 mb-4 p-3 color-border-info color-bg-info f5"> 
          <p><strong>Tip:</strong> When you @mention an organization, only those that you're a member of will autocomplete. You can still @mention organizations that you're not a member of, like a previous employer, but the organization name won't autocomplete for you.</p> 
         </div> </li> 
        <li> <p>Click <strong>Update profile</strong>. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/update-profile-button.png" alt="Update profile button"></span></p> </li> 
       </ol> 
       <h3 id="setting-a-status"><a href="#setting-a-status">Setting a status</a></h3> 
       <p>You can set a status to display information about your current availability on GitHub. Your status will show:</p> 
       <ul> 
        <li>on your GitHub profile page.</li> 
        <li>when people hover over your username or avatar on GitHub.</li> 
        <li>on a team page for a team where you're a team member. For more information, see "<a href="/en/articles/about-teams/#team-pages">About teams</a>."</li> 
        <li>on the organization dashboard in an organization where you're a member. For more information, see "<a href="/en/articles/about-your-organization-dashboard">About your organization dashboard</a>."</li> 
       </ul> 
       <p>When you set your status, you can also let people know that you have limited availability on GitHub.</p> 
       <p><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/username-with-limited-availibilty-text.png" alt="At-mentioned username shows &quot;busy&quot; note next to username"></p> 
       <p><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/request-a-review-limited-availability-status.png" alt="Requested reviewer shows &quot;busy&quot; note next to username"></p> 
       <p>If you select the "Busy" option, when people @mention your username, assign you an issue or pull request, or request a pull request review from you, a note next to your username will show that you're busy.</p> 
       <ol> 
        <li>In the top right corner of GitHub, click your profile photo, then click <strong>Set your status</strong> or, if you already have a status set, click your current status. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/set-status-on-profile.png" alt="Button on profile to set your status"></span></li> 
        <li>To add custom text to your status, click in the text field and type a status message. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/type-a-status-message.png" alt="Field to type a status message"></span></li> 
        <li>Optionally, to set an emoji status, click the smiley icon and select an emoji from the list. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/select-emoji-status.png" alt="Button to select an emoji status"></span></li> 
        <li>Optionally, if you'd like to share that you have limited availability, select "Busy." <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/limited-availability-status.png" alt="Busy option selected in Edit status options"></span></li> 
        <li>Use the <strong>Clear status</strong> drop-down menu, and select when you want your status to expire. If you don't select a status expiration, you will keep your status until you clear or edit your status. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/status-expiration.png" alt="Drop down menu to choose when your status expires"></span></li> 
        <li>Use the drop-down menu and click the organization you want your status visible to. If you don't select an organization, your status will be public. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/status-visibility.png" alt="Drop down menu to choose who your status is visible to"></span></li> 
        <li>Click <strong>Set status</strong>. <span class="procedural-image-wrapper"><img src="/assets/images/help/profile/set-status-button.png" alt="Button to set status"></span></li> 
       </ol>
