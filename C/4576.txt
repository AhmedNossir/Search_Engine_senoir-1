      <p>A <b>Wiki<a href="/wiki/Sloth" title="Sloth">Sloth</a></b> is a user who makes contributions based solely on <a href="/wiki/Hedonism" title="Hedonism">hedonistic</a> intellectual enjoyment. Their contributions are valuable because they rarely get involved in protracted disputes or edit wars, due to their attitude usually being that of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DGAF" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DGAF">WP:DGAF</a>. </p>
      <p>WikiSloths can be found anywhere, due to their tending to edit as the <a href="/wiki/Mood_(psychology)" title="Mood (psychology)">mood</a> takes them. They will disagree with other users if necessary but not get into a long exchange of nasty comments or personal attacks, as then it stops being fun. </p>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Differences_and_similarities_to_other_Wikifauna">Differences and similarities to other Wikifauna</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:WikiSloth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Differences and similarities to other Wikifauna">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>They mostly resemble the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiOgre" title="Wikipedia:WikiOgre">WikiOgre</a>, depending on how regularly they edit Wikipedia, with short spurts of large edits, complete rewrites, or even new articles, followed by periods of rest fixing minor typos and errors as they find them. But they differ in that they are not inspired so much by passion, as by random <a href="/wiki/Interest_(emotion)" title="Interest (emotion)">interest</a> and inclination. </p>
      <p>WikiSloths will respect, but not feel much kinship with, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiGnome" title="Wikipedia:WikiGnome">WikiGnomes</a>, because the latter carry out what appears to resemble <i>work.</i> They may occasionally be found doing the tasks favoured by the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiElf" title="Wikipedia:WikiElf">WikiElves</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiFairy" title="Wikipedia:WikiFairy">WikiFairies</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiGremlin" title="Wikipedia:WikiGremlin">WikiGremlins</a>, when they have a <a href="/wiki/Fad" title="Fad">fad</a> for the task involved. </p>
      <p>Very rarely do they edit like a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiDragon" title="Wikipedia:WikiDragon">WikiDragon</a>, but this would depend on whether a subject interests them sufficiently. It's not often that a WikiSloth will want to invest such an amount of time and energy into one particular article or protracted edit. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Conflicts_and_friendships_with_other_editors">Conflicts and friendships with other editors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:WikiSloth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Conflicts and friendships with other editors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>WikiSloths may be found on User talk pages because they enjoy collaborating and discussion with other editors. After editing for a while they may turn up in Wikipedia space, on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:ANI" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:ANI">WP:ANI</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:RfA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:RfA">WP:RfA</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:AfD" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:AfD">WP:AfD</a> for the same reason, because they enjoy sharing their opinion. They tend to dislike rude editors, unless they are amusing. </p>
      <p>Their initial edits to an article may invite confrontation from fanatics of the subject involved, simply because they rarely have the same degree of investment in it and are therefore more detached. </p>
      <p>On rare occasions WikiSloths may turn aggressive when their laid-back lifestyles are threatened, for example when attacked by a <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:BADGER" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:BADGER">badger</a>. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Positive_and_negative_qualities">Positive and negative qualities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:WikiSloth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Positive and negative qualities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>All the advantages of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:DGAF" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:DGAF">WP:DGAF</a> apply, with the WikiSloths having a knack for detachment and <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPOV" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPOV">evenhandedness</a>. </p>
      <p>At times, they amuse themselves by lighthearted mocking, especially if they perceive that others are taking themselves too seriously. This same lightheartedness prevents them from criticizing others at length. </p>
      <p>Indeed, it's hard for a WikiSloth to do anything at length. They slowly amble, or neatly swing, from one aspect of the wiki to another, though they often return to articles they are interested in. They may gradually shape those articles to high quality, but they will seldom purposely go through the <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:GA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:GA">Good Article</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:FA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:FA">Featured Article</a> process themselves. It's too much bother, and these laid-back creatures don't care about collecting awards. If discussion in one of their favored topics bogs down into nitpicking or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:LAWYER" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:LAWYER">Wikilawyering</a> a WikiSloth will wander away and return after the dust has settled. </p>
      <p>WikiSloths could be seen as one of the more down-to-earth creatures on Wikipedia, as evinced by their not being named after a <a href="/wiki/Mythical_beast" class="mw-redirect" title="Mythical beast">mythical beast</a>. They can be addicted to Wikipedia, but realize that in the end it's just a hobby rather than a religion. Partly for this reason, they tend to shrug off <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:CIV" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:CIV">harsh language</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NPA" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NPA">personal attacks</a>. Nonetheless, like their namesake they may be armed with sharp claws that can surprise an unwary <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TROLL" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TROLL">predator</a>. </p>
      <p>If they find themselves getting too engrossed on Wikipedia, they may force themselves to engage in some other activities to still have something to chat about with those in the <a href="/wiki/Real_life" title="Real life">real world</a>. The Wikicareer of a WikiSloth can go several ways; either their contributions remain occasional or become more so, or they find Wikipedia a very enjoyable hobby and become a frequent editor. They also may go through spates of editing, or move on to their next enthusiasm. </p> 
      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="How_to_identify_a_WikiSloth">How to identify a WikiSloth</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikipedia:WikiSloth&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: How to identify a WikiSloth">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>You may spot a WikiSloth by their lighthearted or laid back attitude to editing. Or look for the Userbox {{<a href="/wiki/Template:User_wikipedia/WikiSloth" title="Template:User wikipedia/WikiSloth">User wikipedia/WikiSloth</a>}} on a User's userpage, or place this on your own. </p>
      <p>A WikiSloth who is feeling particularly active long enough to do so may identify him/herself by displaying a sloth at the top of the page next to the user's name. The following code should produce a small sloth at the top of the page: <b>{{<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiSloth/topicon" title="Wikipedia:WikiSloth/topicon">Wikipedia:WikiSloth/topicon</a>}}</b>. </p>
