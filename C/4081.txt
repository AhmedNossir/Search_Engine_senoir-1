       <p>Could anybody help to check whether the 2003 copyright law of Sri Lanka was retroactive or not? I see nothing about this in <a href="/wiki/Commons:Sri_Lanka" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:Sri Lanka">COM:Sri Lanka</a> but the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-U.S._copyrights#Dates_of_restoration_and_terms_of_protection" class="extiw" title="wikipedia:Non-U.S. copyrights">English Wikipedia page</a> suggests that expired copyright were not restored by 2003 law (eg. they state that pre-1978 photos are no longer copyrighted).          @<a href="/wiki/User:Glorious_93" title="User:Glorious 93">Glorious 93</a>: Commons generally takes the position that making a faithful digital copy of a two-dimensional work doesn't create a new copyright. So if the underlying work is definitely out of copyright, and no publication right applies, and the picture includes nothing other than the underlying work (no frame, for instance), then you can ignore any claimed copyright on the digitised version. The <a href="/wiki/Template:PD-scan" title="Template:PD-scan"><span style="font-family:monospace,monospace;">{{PD-scan}}</span></a> and <a href="/wiki/Template:PD-art" class="mw-redirect" title="Template:PD-art"><span style="font-family:monospace,monospace;">{{PD-art}}</span></a> templates are intended for this kind of situation.        <p>Hello! I uploaded photos of my license plate collection to the Commons, to be used on their respective pages. However, I got a 7 day deletion notice saying they’ll be deleted if I don’t provide the correct copyright info (look at my user talk page for more info). However, I already have a Creative Commons Attribution-Share Alike 4.0 International license on all of them. What do I need to do to prevent them from being deleted? Thanks! <small lang="en" dir="ltr">—&nbsp;Preceding <a href="/wiki/Commons:Signatures" title="Commons:Signatures">unsigned</a> comment added by <bdi><a href="/wiki/User:RobertCars824" title="User:RobertCars824">RobertCars824</a></bdi> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:RobertCars824" title="User talk:RobertCars824">talk</a>&nbsp;•&nbsp;<a href="/wiki/Special:Contributions/RobertCars824" title="Special:Contributions/RobertCars824">contribs</a>) 20:32, 4 June 2021 (UTC)</small> </p>

         @<a href="/wiki/User:RobertCars824" title="User:RobertCars824">RobertCars824</a>: The issue is that there are two copyrights that apply to these files. You, the photographer, hold the copyright over your photographs of these plates. Additionally, there is copyright over the designs of the plates, which is held by the states (or whoever designed the plates). In order for the photographs of the plates to be eligible to be hosted on Commons, we must have free usability on both of these fronts. While you have provided information on the license that you applied to the photographs, you have not provided sufficient information for the design of the license plates. Unfortunately, it is very likely that the license plate designs are not freely licensed or in the public domain, so they may be deleted. <b style="line-height:1.1;display:inline-block;transform:skew(-14deg);border-radius:2em;border:solid#34c 1px;overflow:hidden"><a href="/wiki/User:Mysterymanblue" title="User:Mysterymanblue"><b style="color:#fff;background:#34c">?Mysteryman</b></a><a href="/wiki/User_talk:Mysterymanblue" title="User talk:Mysterymanblue">blue?</a></b> 20:51, 4 June 2021 (UTC)

             <div class="paragraphbreak" style="margin-top:0.5em"></div> As for your question, there are lots of people who upload files to Commons who mean well, but you might not fully understand <a href="/wiki/Commons:Licensing" title="Commons:Licensing">Commons:Licensing</a>; so, they make a mistake similar to the one you made. With respect to US state license plates, you might want to take a look at the information in <a href="/wiki/Commons:TOO" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:TOO">COM:TOO</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commons:PD" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:PD">COM:PD</a>. In some cases, the state which issues the plate might have passed legislation that designates all content created by its employees as part of their official duties to be within in the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WP:PD" class="extiw" title="en:WP:PD">public domain</a> and thus ineligible for copyright protection regardless of how complex it might be.
