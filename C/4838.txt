      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tagging_pages_for_speedy_deletion">Tagging pages for speedy deletion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=User_talk:PohranicniStraze&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tagging pages for speedy deletion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <p>Hello, PohranicniStraze, </p>
      <p>First, thanks for your editing. But I have one request. Could you use <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:TWINKLE" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:TWINKLE">Twinkle</a> when you tag pages for deletion? It's really easy and, once you set up your Preferences to "Notify page creator", Twinkle will post a notice of your page tagging on the talk page of the page creator. This is an important step that doesn't happen when you just put a deletion tag on a page and if you skip it, the page creator has no idea what has happened to their work because only admins can view Deleted Contributions. Even for pages that really deserve deletion, it's a courtesy to let page creators know what has happened. And with Twinkle, the program does all of the work for you. Please consider it. Thank you. <span style="font-family:Papyrus; color:#800080;"><a href="/wiki/User:Liz" title="User:Liz"><i><b>L</b></i>iz</a></span> <sup style="font-family: Times New Roman; color: #006400;"><a href="/wiki/Special:Contributions/Liz" title="Special:Contributions/Liz"><i><b>Read!</b></i></a> <a href="/wiki/User_talk:Liz" title="User talk:Liz"><i><b>Talk!</b></i></a></sup> 01:37, 28 May 2021 (UTC) </p>
