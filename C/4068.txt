           This help desk is a forum for questions and help on:
          </div>
          <div style="font-size: 2.5em; font-weight: bold;">
           How to use Commons
          </div>
         </div> 
         <p>Anyone, from newbie to experienced, can ask a question here. Questions will be replied to here as well. Any answers you receive are not legal advice and the responder cannot be held liable for them. </p> 
         <div class="center"> 
          <p><span class="plainlinks"><a class="external text" href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?title=Commons:Help_desk&amp;action=edit&amp;section=new"><span class="mw-ui-button mw-ui-progressive" role="button" style="">Ask your question</span></a></span> </p>

         <p>In order to get quick answers consider the following points: </p> 
         <ul>
          <li>Have you checked the <b><a href="/wiki/Commons:FAQ" title="Commons:FAQ">frequently asked questions</a></b>, <b><a href="/wiki/Help:Contents" title="Help:Contents">help pages</a></b> and the <b><a href="/wiki/Commons:Help_desk/Archives" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:Help desk/Archives">help desk archive</a></b>?</li>
         </ul> 
         <ul>
          <li>For <b>licensing and copyright issues,</b> please use the <a href="/wiki/Commons:Village_pump/Copyright" title="Commons:Village pump/Copyright">copyright village pump</a></li> 
          <li>For <b>proposing changes</b> to the way Wikimedia Commons works, please use the <a href="/wiki/Commons:Village_pump/Proposals" title="Commons:Village pump/Proposals">proposals village pump</a></li> 
          <li>For <b>community-wide discussions,</b> please use the <a href="/wiki/Commons:Village_pump" title="Commons:Village pump">main village pump</a></li> 
          <li>For <b>changing the name of a file,</b> please see our <a href="/wiki/Commons:File_renaming" title="Commons:File renaming">file renaming guidelines</a></li> 
          <li>For <b>matters requiring the attention of an administrator,</b> please use the <a href="/wiki/Commons:Administrators%27_noticeboard" title="Commons:Administrators' noticeboard">administrators' noticeboard</a> or contact an individual <a href="/wiki/Commons:List_of_administrators" title="Commons:List of administrators">administrator</a> directly</li> 
          <li>For questions <b>not related to this website,</b> try <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Reference_desk" class="extiw" title="w:Wikipedia:Reference desk">English Wikipedia's reference desk</a></li>
         </ul> 
         <p>Resolved sections (marked by <code>{{section resolved|1=~~~~}}</code>) will be archived after two days. Sections with no discussion will be archived after ten days. <br> </p>

       <p>I used a diagram that had non-English text and <a href="/wiki/File:OLAP_slicing_en.png" title="File:OLAP slicing en.png">recreated</a> it with English text. At the time of uploading, the wizard asked me if it was my Own Work, and I selected yes, since I worked on it. The wizard didn't ask me for a source. Now in the summary, in the Source field it says "Own work" and I had to mention the actual source in the Description. </p>
       <p>In order to be clear on what Own Work means, I checked up <a href="/wiki/Commons:Own_work" title="Commons:Own work">Commons:Own work</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commons:Derivative_works" title="Commons:Derivative works">Commons:Derivative works</a>, and the FAQs and Help Desk archives, yet I do not get an idea on what it means for a modified diagram on Commons. <a href="/wiki/Commons:Help_desk/Archive/2015/10#Want_to_upload_an_image_containing_multiple_works_on_Wikimedia_commons" title="Commons:Help desk/Archive/2015/10">One of the archived conversations</a> suggests that modifications that are "worked on" are to be treated as Own work. Kindly clarify. <a href="/wiki/User:Jay" title="User:Jay">Jay</a> (<a href="/wiki/User_talk:Jay" title="User talk:Jay"><span class="signature-talk">talk</span></a>) 07:22, 23 May 2021 (UTC) </p>

I uploaded my <a href="/wiki/File:OLAP_dicing_en.png" title="File:OLAP dicing en.png">next modified file</a> under the misleading option "This file is not my own work" (since there is no option for derivative work in the wizard). The only issue with that would be if you made modifications that the original copyright-holder wouldn't want anyone to confuse as being theirs. Example: <a href="/wiki/File:Asahel_Curtis_panorama_of_Fremont_district_and_north_end_of_Lake_Union_from_Queen_Anne_Hill_(Seattle,_1902).jpg" title="File:Asahel Curtis panorama of Fremont district and north end of Lake Union from Queen Anne Hill (Seattle, 1902).jpg">File:Asahel Curtis panorama of Fremont district and north end of Lake Union from Queen Anne Hill (Seattle, 1902).jpg</a>, so people could treat this as public domain (which was the status of the underlying photos).

       <p>UNHCR's has build a first of a kind Connecting Worlds app, where refugees and donors, in a secure environment, can exchange messages and images to create a stronger connection and common understanding. UNHCR, the UN Refugee Agency, is mandated to lead and coordinate international action for the worldwide protection of refugees and the resolution of refugee problems. The APP is in a pilot phase and has been tested in three regions with very positive reviews from both refugees and donors. </p>
