      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Wikimedia Commons is a <b>media file repository</b> making available public domain and freely-licensed educational media content (images, sound and video clips) to all. It acts as a common repository for all <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/gl:Fundaci%C3%B3n_Wikimedia" class="extiw" title="w:gl:Fundación Wikimedia">Wikimedia</a> projects, but the content can be used by anyone, anywhere, for any purpose. </p>

       <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aim_of_Wikimedia_Commons">Aim of Wikimedia Commons</span></h2> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>The aim of Wikimedia Commons is to provide a media file repository: </p> 
       <ul>
        <li>that makes available public domain and freely-licensed educational media content to all, and</li> 
        <li>that acts as a common repository for the various projects of the Wikimedia Foundation.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>The expression "educational" is to be understood according to its broad meaning of "<i>providing knowledge; instructional or informative</i>". </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Scope_of_Commons"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scope_of_Commons">Scope of Commons</span></h2> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>To be eligible for hosting on Wikimedia Commons, all files and other content must fall within the Commons scope. Anything uploaded here which falls outside this scope will be deleted as <b>OOS</b> (Out Of Scope). </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Excluded_educational_content"></span><span id="EXED"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Excluded_educational_content">Excluded educational content</span></h3> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Certain content is excluded from Commons, not because it is intrinsically non-educational, but because there are other Wikimedia Foundation projects that are more appropriate for hosting such content. This applies both to media files and to text. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Excluded educational content includes: </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Encyclopedia articles; these may be hosted on <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/" class="extiw" title="w:">Wikipedia</a>.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>News (this may be hosted on <a href="https://en.wikinews.org/wiki/" class="extiw" title="n:">Wikinews</a>), general weather reports, and the like.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Files that contain nothing educational other than raw text. Purely textual material such as plain-text versions of recipes, lists of instructions, poetry, fiction, quotations, dictionary definitions, lesson plans or classroom material, and the like are better hosted elsewhere, for example at <a href="https://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/" class="extiw" title="b:">Wikibooks</a>, <a href="https://en.wikiquote.org/wiki/" class="extiw" title="q:">Wikiquote</a>, <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/" class="extiw" title="wikt:">Wiktionary</a>, <a href="https://en.wikiversity.org/wiki/" class="extiw" title="v:">Wikiversity</a> or <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/" class="extiw" title="s:">Wikisource</a>.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>However, Commons can be used to host such material if included in a shareable media file that is of use to one of the other Wikimedia Foundation-hosted (WMF) projects, so scanned copies of existing texts that are useful to other WMF projects (e.g. to serve as the basis of a reliable, verifiable source) are in scope. Also allowed are files which embody something of value over and above raw text. For example, files consisting of scans of out-of-copyright books, newspapers and the like which preserve original font, layout, embedded images and the like are within scope. </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Scope_part_1:_Files"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scope_part_1:_Files">Scope part 1: Files</span></h2> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Uploaded files are within scope only if they comply with all of the following conditions. Every file: </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Must be a media file.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Must be of an allowable free file format.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Must be freely licensed or public domain.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Must be realistically useful for an educational purpose.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Must not contain only <a href="#Excluded_educational_content">excluded educational content</a>.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>These are explained in more detail below. </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Must_be_a_media_file"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Must_be_a_media_file">Must be a media file</span></h3> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Wikimedia Commons hosts only media files such as photographs, scanned images, diagrams, animations, audio (e.g. music and spoken dialogue) and video clips, along with any associated metadata. Explanatory and other text is permitted on the file page only to the extent to which it advances <a href="#commons-aims">Commons' aim</a> and is not <a href="#Excluded_educational_content">excluded educational content</a>. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>The following are not considered media files, and may not be hosted here: </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Computer programs in any format including binary executable files and raw source code listings. Where relevant, source-code may form part of the file description or metadata, e.g. a graphics file may include as descriptive text the code used to create it.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Files which are representative merely of raw text, e.g. ASCII files, raw source code listings as mentioned above, etc.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Must_be_an_allowable_free_file_format"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Must_be_an_allowable_free_file_format">Must be an allowable free file format</span></h3> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Only free file formats are allowed. Proprietary formats such as Microsoft <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/gl:Microsoft_Word" class="extiw" title="w:gl:Microsoft Word">Word</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/gl:Microsoft_Excel" class="extiw" title="w:gl:Microsoft Excel">Excel</a> documents, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/gl:Advanced_Audio_Coding" class="extiw" title="w:gl:Advanced Audio Coding">AAC</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/pt:Windows_Media_Audio" class="extiw" title="w:pt:Windows Media Audio">WMA</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High_Efficiency_Video_Coding" class="extiw" title="w:High Efficiency Video Coding">H.265</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High_Efficiency_Image_File_Format" class="extiw" title="w:High Efficiency Image File Format">HEIC</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/gl:Flash_Video" class="extiw" title="w:gl:Flash Video">Flash</a> and the like are not permitted. More generally, any file that requires an unfree program or patent-encumbered codec in order to access its content is prohibited. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>A list of currently allowed file formats can be found at <a href="/wiki/Commons:Project_scope/Allowable_file_types/gl" title="Commons:Project scope/Allowable file types/gl">Commons:Project scope/Allowable file types/gl</a>. </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Must_be_freely_licensed_or_public_domain"></span><span id="FREEORPD"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Must_be_freely_licensed_or_public_domain">Must be freely licensed or public domain</span></h3> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Wikimedia Commons accepts only <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/gl:Contido_libre" class="extiw" title="w:gl:Contido libre">free content</a>, in other words files that are either freely licensed or which are in the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/gl:Dominio_p%C3%BAblico" class="extiw" title="w:gl:Dominio público">public domain</a>. A file is considered public domain if either all copyright has expired or if the copyright owner(s) has voluntarily placed the content of the file into the public domain by irrevocably renouncing all copyright. A file which is ineligible for copyright protection is also considered public domain. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Any file hosted here must normally be freely licensed or public domain according to both the law of the United States and according to the law of the source country, if different: see <a href="/wiki/Commons:Licensing/gl" title="Commons:Licensing/gl">Commons:Licenzamento</a>. </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Required_licensing_terms"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Required_licensing_terms">Required licensing terms</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>To be considered freely licensed, the copyright owner has to release the file under an irrevocable licence which: </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Permits free reuse for any purpose, including commercial.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Permits the creation of derivative works.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Non-allowable_licence_terms"></span><span id="NONALLOW"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-allowable_licence_terms">Non-allowable licence terms</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>The following licensing terms are not allowed: </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Non-commercial or educational use only.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Restrictions on the creation of <a href="/wiki/Commons:Derivative_works/gl" title="Commons:Derivative works/gl">derivative works</a>, except for <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/gl:Copyleft" class="extiw" title="w:gl:Copyleft">copyleft</a>.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>A requirement for payment or for notification of use; these can be <i>requested</i> but not required.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Restrictions on where the work may be used, e.g. use allowed on Wikipedia only.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Licences with these restrictions are allowed as long as the work is <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/pt:Licen%C3%A7a_dual" class="extiw" title="w:pt:Licença dual">dual-licensed or multi-licensed</a> with at least one licensing option that does not include such a restriction. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>"Licences" which purport to allow <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/gl:Uso_lex%C3%ADtimo" class="extiw" title="w:gl:Uso lexítimo">fair use</a> only are not allowed. Fair use is not a right that can be licensed by a copyright owner, and is in any event <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Commons:Fair_use" title="Special:MyLanguage/Commons:Fair use">never accepted on Commons</a>. </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Allowable_licence_terms"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Allowable_licence_terms">Allowable licence terms</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>The following terms are allowed: </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>A requirement that the copyright owner(s) be named ("attribution").</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>A requirement that any derivative works must be licensed under the same terms as the original ("share-alike"/"copyleft").</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Licensing policy is defined in detail at <a href="/wiki/Commons:Licensing/gl" title="Commons:Licensing/gl">Commons:Licenzamento</a>. </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Evidence"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Evidence">Evidence</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="gl" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
        <p>In all cases the uploader must provide appropriate evidence to demonstrate either that the file is in the public domain or that the copyright owner has released it under a suitable licence. Typically that requires at least that the source of the file be specified,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> along with the original source where the file is a <a href="/wiki/Commons:Derivative_works/gl" title="Commons:Derivative works/gl">derivative work</a>. Also, the creator or copyright owner should be identified, if known or reasonably ascertainable. If there is any question, evidence may need to be supplied that the copyright owner has indeed released the file under the given licence. </p> 
       </div> 
       <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
        <p>Where the file is a photograph which shows an identifiable person, the subject's consent may be required as described at <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Commons:Photographs_of_identifiable_people" title="Special:MyLanguage/Commons:Photographs of identifiable people">Commons:Photographs of identifiable people</a>. </p> 
       </div> 
       <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
        <p>In all cases, the burden of proof lies on the uploader or other person arguing for the file to be retained to demonstrate that as far as can reasonably be determined: </p> 
       </div> 
       <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
        <ul>
         <li>the file is in the public domain or is properly licensed, and</li> 
         <li>that any required consent has been obtained.</li>
        </ul> 
       </div> 
       <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
        <p>In the case of an old file, date and country of publication can help determine if the file is in the public domain due to age. </p> 
       </div>

      <p><span id="PRP"></span><span id="Precautionary_principle"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Precautionary_principle">Precautionary principle</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <p>Commons' users aim to build and maintain in good faith a repository of media files which to the best of our knowledge are free or freely-licensed. <b>The precautionary principle</b> is that where there is <i>significant</i> doubt about the freedom of a particular file, it should be deleted. </p>
      <p>Also, arguments that amount to "we can get away with it", such as the following, are against Commons' aims: </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>"<i>The copyright owner will not bother to sue or cannot afford to.</i>"</li> 
       <li>"<i>The copyright owner will never find out.</i>"</li> 
       <li>"<i>The copyright owner will not mind/should be pleased that we have disseminated their work.</i>"</li> 
       <li>"<i>Nobody knows who the copyright owner is, so it really doesn’t matter.</i>"</li> 
       <li>"<i>The file is obviously common property. It can be found all over the internet and nobody has complained.</i>"</li>
      </ol>

      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Must_be_realistically_useful_for_an_educational_purpose">Must be realistically useful for an educational purpose</span></h3> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>The expression "educational" is to be understood according to its broad meaning of "<i>providing knowledge; instructional or informative</i>". </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>In the sections below, any use that is not made in good faith does not count. For example, images that are being used on a talk page just to make a point can be discounted. </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="File_in_use_in_another_Wikimedia_project"></span><span id="in_use"></span></p>
      <div class="shortcut2" style="">
       <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r433650343"><a href="/wiki/Commons:Shortcuts" title="Commons:Shortcuts">Shortcut</a>
       <br><a href="/wiki/Commons:INUSE" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:INUSE">COM:INUSE</a>
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="File_in_use_in_another_Wikimedia_project">File in use in another Wikimedia project</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>A media file that is in use on one of the other projects of the Wikimedia Foundation is considered automatically to be useful for an educational purpose, as is a file in use for some operational reason such as within a template or the like. Such a file is not liable to deletion simply because it may be of poor quality: if it is in use, that is enough. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>The uploading of small numbers of images (e.g. of yourself) for use on a personal user page of Commons or another project is allowed as long as that user is or was an active participant on that project. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>It should be stressed that Commons does not overrule other projects about what is in scope. If an image is in use on another project (aside from use on talk pages or user pages), that is enough for it to be within scope. </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="File_in_use_on_Commons_only"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="File_in_use_on_Commons_only">File in use on Commons only</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>An otherwise non-educational file does not acquire educational purpose solely because it is in use on a gallery page or in a category on Commons, nor solely because it is in use on a user page (the "User:" namespace), but by custom the uploading of small numbers of images (e.g. of yourself) for use on a personal Commons user page is allowed. Files relating to projects or events of the Wikimedia community, such as user meetings, are also allowed. </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="File_not_legitimately_in_use"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="File_not_legitimately_in_use">File not legitimately in use</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div class="shortcut2" style="">
       <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r433650343"><a href="/wiki/Commons:Shortcuts" title="Commons:Shortcuts">Shortcut</a>
       <br><a href="/wiki/Commons:NOTUSED" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:NOTUSED">COM:NOTUSED</a>
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Not_used"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>A media file which is neither: </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>realistically useful for an educational purpose, nor</li> 
        <li>legitimately in use as discussed above</li>
       </ul> 
       <p>falls outside the scope of Wikimedia Commons. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>The emphasis here is on realistic utility, either for one of the Wikimedia projects or for some other educational use. Not all images for example are realistically useful for an educational purpose. An image does not magically become useful by virtue of the argument that it could be used to illustrate a Wikipedia article on <i>X</i>, merely because <i>X</i> happens to be the subject of the photograph. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>For example, the fact that an unused blurred photograph could theoretically be used to illustrate an article on "Common mistakes in photography" does not mean that we should keep all blurred photographs. The fact that an unused snapshot of your friend could theoretically be used to illustrate an article on "Photographic portraiture" does not mean that we should keep all photographs of unknown people. The fact that an unused pornographic image could theoretically be used to illustrate an article on pornography does not mean that we should keep low quality pornographic images (see also <a href="#Censorship">Censorship</a>). </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Excluded from this policy are files in different formats and files which are similar but not exact duplicates in cases where they are needed to keep the file history for legal reasons. </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Examples"></span> </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Exemplos">Exemplos</span></h4> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Examples of files that are not realistically useful for an educational purpose: </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <div class="shortcut2" style="">
        <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r433650343"><a href="/wiki/Commons:Shortcuts" title="Commons:Shortcuts">Shortcuts</a>
        <br><a href="/wiki/Commons:SPAM" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:SPAM">COM:SPAM</a>
        <br><a href="/wiki/Commons:PARTYPICS" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:PARTYPICS">COM:PARTYPICS</a>
       </div> 
       <ul>
        <li><span id="PARTYPICS"></span>Private image collections, e.g. private party photos, photos of yourself and your friends, your collection of holiday snaps and so on. There are plenty of other projects on the Internet you can use for such a purpose, such as <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/gl:Flickr" class="extiw" title="w:gl:Flickr">Flickr</a>. Such private image collections do not become educational even if displayed as a gallery on a user page on Commons or elsewhere.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Artwork without obvious educational use, including non-educational artwork uploaded to showcase the artist's skills</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Files apparently created and/or uploaded for the purpose of vandalism or attack. Preexisting designs and symbols that are or have been associated with nationalistic, religious or racist causes are not out of scope solely because they may cause offence. Provided they are legal to host and otherwise fall within Commons scope (e.g. if they could for example be used to illustrate a Wikipedia article on a hate group), they should be kept.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Advertising or self-promotion.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>Files that add nothing educationally distinct to the collection of images we already hold covering the same subject, especially if they are of poor or mediocre quality.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Discussion"></span> </p> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Conversa">Conversa</span></h4> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>We hold many high-quality images of species-identified birds, and there is no realistic educational use for a small, blurry, poorly composed snapshot of an unidentified and unidentifiable bird. Of course, there is always room for another educationally distinct image, for example illustrating some aspect of bird behaviour that we do not currently cover, even if the image is perhaps not of the highest quality. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>There may sometimes be an argument for retaining multiple images that are quite similar from an educational point of view, for the sake of variety and availability of choice, but there is no purpose in our hosting many essentially identical poor-quality images that have no realistic educational value. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>New educational files of exceptional quality are always welcome, and the later uploading of such files may in principle render earlier unused poor quality files educationally redundant. However, as indicated above, a file that is used in good faith on a Wikimedia project is always considered educational, so a poor-quality file that remains in use is not eligible for deletion even if a better-quality file covering the same subject later becomes available. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>New and existing files of poor or mediocre quality may or may not be realistically useful for an educational purpose depending on what they illustrate and what other files we have of the same subject. Where a subject is rare and/or difficult to capture, even a poor-quality file may be of significant educational value, especially if Commons has very few or no similar files already. On the other hand, poor or mediocre files of common and easy-to-capture subjects may have no realistic educational value, especially if Commons already hosts many similar or better quality examples. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Image quality is just one of the factors that may limit the educational usefulness of a file. Other limiting factors may include low resolution and hard-to-remove watermarks. </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="PDF_and_DjVu_formats"></span><span id="PDF"></span><span id="DjVu"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="PDF_and_DjVu_formats">PDF and DjVu formats</span></h3> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Although PDF and DjVu file formats are permitted, they are expected to be used only in appropriate cases. There should be some reason for the choice of format that is in line with <a href="#commons-aims">Commons' aims</a>. All of the above rules apply as well, of course. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>An admin considering whether to delete a PDF or DjVu file may have to exercise judgement as to whether the chosen format is in line with Commons' aims, and the context and intent (if known) may be relevant. For example, while a published university thesis in PDF format may be OK, a user-created original-research article that is making use of Commons as a free web-host may not be. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Files that might realistically be useful to one or more other Wikimedia projects, e.g. Wikisource or Wikibooks, should be kept; deletions should not be based on the sole ground that the file would be better hosted on one of those other projects. Any media file that is realistically useful to or is within the scope of even one other Wikimedia Foundation project can be hosted here. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Remember that Wikisource often needs PDF or DjVu files in order to proofread or create source texts: Therefore, scans of suitable editions of notable public domain works are <i>almost always within scope</i> for this reason. That said, remember that editorial decisions involved in preparing a text from several sources may result in a new copyright, so the editions used <i>must</i> be out of copyright themselves. </p> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Allowable_reasons_for_PDF_and_DjVu_formats"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Allowable_reasons_for_PDF_and_DjVu_formats">Allowable reasons for PDF and DjVu formats</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>The format has been selected for convenience of printing.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>The format has been selected to allow viewing or printing of special fonts, e.g. documents in advanced mathematics or linguistics.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>The file would be within the scope of another project of the Wikimedia Foundation if it were to be uploaded in the same format to that project, for example:</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>
         <ul>
          <li>A PDF or DjVu file of a published and peer-reviewed work would be in scope on Wikisource and is therefore also in scope on Commons. Examples of in-scope documents include published books (but not vanity publishing), peer-reviewed academic papers, etc., university theses and dissertations.</li>
         </ul></li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>
         <ul>
          <li>The file is a scan of a document of historic or other external significance, e.g. scans of existing copyright-free or licensed books, reports, newspapers, etc.</li> 
          <li>The file is usable as a fixed, verifiable source document, e.g. for Wikisource or Wikibooks (appropriate fixation of content).</li>
         </ul></li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>
         <ul>
          <li>The format selected provides technical advantages to at least one other Wikimedia Foundation project.</li>
         </ul></li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Non-allowable_reasons_for_PDF_and_DjVu_formats"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-allowable_reasons_for_PDF_and_DjVu_formats">Non-allowable reasons for PDF and DjVu formats</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <ul>
        <li>The format has apparently been selected by the author or uploader to prevent or to discourage the creation of derivative works contrary to <a href="#commons-aims">Commons' aims</a>. Such inappropriate fixation of content would include a self-published vanity article or a self-created image that the author has uploaded in PDF format in an effort to discourage others from creating derivatives.</li> 
        <li>The content is essentially raw text; such files are not considered media files. Note that scans of existing books, reports, newspapers etc of historic or other external significance are not excluded on this ground, even if they contain no images.</li> 
        <li>The content would be prohibited under another section; for example, promotional material is outside Commons' scope regardless of the file format.</li>
       </ul> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Scope_part_2:_Pages,_galleries_and_categories"></span><span id="Scope_part_2"></span><span id="galleries"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h2><span id="Scope_part_2:_Pages.2C_galleries_and_categories"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Scope_part_2:_Pages,_galleries_and_categories">Scope part 2: Pages, galleries and categories</span></h2> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>See <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Commons:Project_scope/Pages,_galleries_and_categories" title="Special:MyLanguage/Commons:Project scope/Pages, galleries and categories">Commons:Project scope/Pages, galleries and categories</a>. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="A_word_on_some_areas_of_particular_concern">A word on some areas of particular concern</span></h2> 
      </div> 
      <p><span id="Censorship"></span><span id="CENSOR"></span> </p> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Censorship">Censorship</span></h3> 
      </div> 
      <div class="shortcut2" style="">
       <link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r433650343"><a href="/wiki/Commons:Shortcuts" title="Commons:Shortcuts">Shortcuts</a>
       <br><a href="/wiki/Commons:CENSOR" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:CENSOR">COM:CENSOR</a>
       <br><a href="/wiki/Commons:CENSORSHIP" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:CENSORSHIP">COM:CENSORSHIP</a>
       <br><a href="/wiki/Commons:OMGAPENIS" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:OMGAPENIS">COM:OMGAPENIS</a>
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Files and other materials which are not lawful for Commons to host on its servers in Virginia will be deleted immediately upon being identified as unlawful (including copyright violations), even if the material otherwise falls within Commons scope, as set out above. However, Commons is not censored, and legitimately includes content which some users may consider objectionable or offensive. The policy of <i>"Commons is not censored"</i> means that a lawfully-hosted file, which falls within Commons' definitions of scope, will not be deleted solely on the grounds that it may not be "child-friendly" or that it may cause offence to you or others, for moral, personal, religious, social, or other reasons. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>The counterpoint to this, is that the statement <i>"Commons is not censored"</i> is not a valid argument for keeping a file that falls outside Commons' defined scope, as set out above. Photographs of nudity including male and female genitalia are sometimes uploaded for non-educational motives, and such images are not exempt from the requirement to comply with the rules of Commons' scope. If the images are of demonstrably inferior quality, or add nothing educationally distinct to the stock of such images we hold already, they may fail the test of being realistically useful for an educational purpose. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>A balance has to be struck between accepting useful media files with legitimate educational content that some may find offensive, and not allowing Commons to be used as a general-purpose media-hosting service (like Flickr, Photobucket, YouTube, etc.), without regard for the project's stated goals. The purpose of Commons is to serve as a media repository, a reliable resource of useful, open source media content; organized and comprehensive in coverage (with accurate file descriptions/information), educational, and intended both for use by Wikimedia projects, and as a public service freely accessible to everyone. </p> 
      </div>
