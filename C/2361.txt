          <hr>
          <h2 id="h_22e18701-3a56-4404-9e22-897c75301174">Overview</h2>
          <p>To route web traffic through the Cloudflare network, update the nameservers at your domain registrar to resolve your domain’s DNS with Cloudflare's nameservers. Updating your nameservers&nbsp;does not change where your website is hosted.&nbsp; Also, you can still use OpenDNS, Google DNS, etc for recursive DNS although Cloudflare recommends the&nbsp;<a href="https://developers.cloudflare.com/1.1.1.1/setting-up-1.1.1.1/">1.1.1.1 resolver</a>.</p>
          <p></p>
          <div class="cf-sidebar-2 cf-note">
           Changing nameservers is not required for Business and Enterprise domains on <a href="https://support.cloudflare.com/hc/en-us/articles/360020348832">CNAME setups</a>. 
          </div>
          <p></p>
          <hr>
          <h2 id="h_17l3jYyLHPIGtMLQW2FwVP">Before you begin</h2>
          <h4><b>Tasks</b></h4>
          <p>Before you change your domain nameservers to Cloudflare, you will need to <a href="https://support.cloudflare.com/hc/articles/201720164">create an account and add your domain to Cloudflare</a>.</p>
          <h4><b>Concepts</b></h4>
          <p>If you are new to Cloudflare or Internet concepts more generally, you might want to review the following concepts:</p>
          <ul>
           <li><a href="https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/dns/glossary/what-is-a-domain-name-registrar/">Registrar</a></li>
           <li><a href="https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/cdn/what-is-a-cdn/">Content Delivery Network (CDN)</a></li>
           <li><a href="https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/dns/dns-server-types">Nameservers</a></li>
           <li><a href="https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/dns/what-is-dns/">Domain Name System (DNS)</a></li>
           <li><a href="https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/dns/dns-security/">DNS Security (DNSSEC)</a></li>
          </ul>
          <p></p>
          <hr>
          <h2 id="434Ce5yZ7oM1ijYPg29SBB">Change your domain nameservers</h2>
          <p>To change your domain nameservers, you will need to use information from Cloudflare to update admin settings at your current registrar.</p>
          <h4><b>1. Copy information from Cloudflare</b></h4>
          <p>Once you have <a href="https://support.cloudflare.com/hc/articles/201720164">added an application</a> to Cloudflare, that application will receive two assigned nameservers. You will need this information for future steps.</p>
          <p>To get this information:</p>
          <p>1. In the <a href="dash.cloudflare.com/?to=/:account/:zone">Cloudflare dashboard</a>, select your account and newly added domain.</p>
          <p>2. Copy the information from <b>Replace with Cloudflare's nameservers</b>.</p>
          <img src="https://images.ctfassets.net/slt3lc6tev37/6td4IKteo4KHdtn7LwiYV8/dd99a297eae1d160d81eb94aaa8c9381/nameserver-names.png" alt="Get the names of your assigned nameservers from the Cloudflare dashboard.">
          <h4><b>2. Update the admin settings for your registrar</b></h4>
          <p>1. If you do not know the name of your registrar, go to <a href="https://whois.icann.org/">ICANN WHOIS</a> and enter your domain.</p>
          <img src="https://images.ctfassets.net/slt3lc6tev37/5RgcL9QrReuwcADbxcdL0A/87bc6a0080fc0ac481cdb0686745d5ec/hc-external_ICANN_whois_domain_nameservers.png" alt="screenshot of ICAN WHOIS page with contact information redacted and registrar and name server section highlighted">
          <p>2. Log into the administrator account for your domain registrar.</p>
          <p>3. Make sure your registrar has disabled DNSSEC for your domain. For more details, see <a href="https://support.cloudflare.com/hc/articles/205359838#h_94453043811540417238269">Why do I have to disable DNSSEC</a><b>.</b> </p>
          <p>4. Replace the current nameserver records in your registrar account with the information you copied from Cloudflare. For detailed instructions, select a link in the list below.</p>

          <p>5. Wait 24 hours while your registrar updates your nameservers. You will receive an email when your site is active on Cloudflare.</p>
          <p>6. Confirm your site activation by logging into the <a href="https://dash.cloudflare.com/?to=/:account/:zone">Cloudflare dashboard</a>:</p>
          <ul>
           <li>If you no longer see&nbsp;<b>Complete your nameserver setup</b>, you have successfully updated your nameservers and your domain is active at Cloudflare.</li>
           <li>If&nbsp;<b>Complete your nameserver setup&nbsp;</b>still appears:
            <ul>
             <li>Ensure the <b>Name Server </b>output correctly spells the Cloudflare nameservers and confirm Cloudflare's nameservers are the only nameservers listed.</li>
             <li>If the <b>Name Server </b>output is correct, select <b>Re-check now</b>.</li>
            </ul></li>
          </ul>

          <div class="cf-sidebar-2 cf-note">
           Certain European registrars have a different nameserver registration process. <a href="https://support.cloudflare.com/hc/en-us/articles/200172476">Contact Cloudflare support</a> if you experience issues. 
          </div>

          <h2 id="Sup6FYrwnD8xnENE3Xrez4nu">How to check if your domain nameservers are now pointing to Cloudflare</h2>
          <p>There are a couple of different methods on how to check whether the nameservers have been updated at your Registrar to point to the Cloudflare nameservers:</p>
          <ul>
           <li>You can use the command line to confirm what are the nameservers assigned to your domain:</li>
          </ul>
          <p></p>
          <pre>*Linux/Unix*
dig domain_name +trace @1.1.1.1 
dig domain_name +trace @8.8.8.8

*Windows*
nslookup domain_name 1.1.1.1
nslookup domain_name 8.8.8.8</pre>
          <p></p>
          <ul>
           <li>You can also use online tools, such as&nbsp;<a href="https://www.whatsmydns.net/">https://www.whatsmydns.net/</a></li>
          </ul>
          <p>If the nameservers returned here are not the ones provided and expected by Cloudflare, you'll need to check with your Registrar.</p>
          <p></p>
          <div class="cf-sidebar-2 cf-note">
           Most DNS tools online use cached query results, therefore it may take longer for them to show the updated nameservers. 
          </div>
          <p></p>
          <p>If you run into issues, below are some things you will want to confirm before reaching out to Support for further assistance:</p>
          <ul>
           <li>check that <a href="https://support.cloudflare.com/hc/en-us/articles/360017421192-Cloudflare-DNS-FAQ#h_3yslZOSrNhsasnFQz7E8T1">no DS record</a> is present, if so remove them at your Registrar</li>
           <li>check that <i><b>only</b></i><i> </i>the Cloudflare nameservers are present, and if not remove the other ones in Registrar configuration</li>
           <li>check that the Cloudflare nameservers are spelled correctly in your Registrar configuration</li>
          </ul>
          <p>Once your domain is active on Cloudflare, review our&nbsp;<a href="https://support.cloudflare.com/hc/articles/360027989951#h_87dac948-9e12-47e0-a32c-2144aadd0a2a">best practices for active Cloudflare domains</a>.</p>
          <hr>
          <h2 id="h_815d1b1e-74cf-4a5f-b477-e441a76ac3fd">Confirm traffic is proxied to Cloudflare</h2>
          <p>Some online tools such as&nbsp;<a href="https://gtmetrix.com/">GTmetrix</a>&nbsp;don't recognize Cloudflare as a Content Delivery Network (<a href="https://www.cloudflare.com/features-cdn">CDN</a>)&nbsp;because we don't operate like a traditional&nbsp;<a href="https://www.cloudflare.com/learning/cdn/what-is-a-cdn/">CDN</a>.&nbsp; Instead, confirm your domain traffic actively proxies through Cloudflare by browsing to&nbsp;https://<i>www.example.com</i>/cdn-cgi/trace.&nbsp;Replace&nbsp;<i>www.example.com</i>&nbsp;with the domain and hostname proxied to Cloudflare.&nbsp;&nbsp;If proxied to Cloudflare, output similar to the following appears in the browser:</p>
          <p></p>
          <pre>  fl=4f177
  h=www.example.com
  ip=2001:1900:2200:7525:749f:9ed1:444c:80b4
  ts=1562191016.292
  visit_scheme=https
  uag=Mozilla/5.0 (Macintosh; Intel Mac OS X 10_12_6) AppleWebKit/537.36 (KHTML,
  like Gecko) Chrome/74.0.3729.157 Safari/537.36
  colo=SJC
  http=http/2
  loc=US
  tls=TLSv1.3
  sni=plaintext
  warp=off
</pre>
          <p></p>
          <p>If you don't observe similar output:</p>
          <ol>
           <li>Confirm your DNS record is <a href="https://support.cloudflare.com/hc/articles/360019093151#h_70577325041543271574371">orange-clouded</a>&nbsp;in the Cloudflare <b>DNS </b>app,</li>
           <li>Enter your domain at&nbsp;<a href="https://whois.icann.org/">ICANN WHOIS</a>&nbsp;to confirm the <b>Name Servers </b>only list Cloudflare nameservers for your domain, or</li>
           <li><a href="https://support.cloudflare.com/hc/articles/200172476">Contact Cloudflare Support</a>.</li>
          </ol>
