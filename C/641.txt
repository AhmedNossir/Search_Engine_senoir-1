      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Modularity_principle">Modularity principle</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>The page (file, category) should be put in the most specific category/categories that fit(s) the page (not directly to its parent categories). A category can have more parent categories. A category can combine two (or more) different criteria; such categories are called "compound categories" or "intersection categories".       <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Simplicity_principle">Simplicity principle</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>This principle suggests not to combine too many different criteria. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Selectivity_principle">Selectivity principle</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>We should not classify items which are related to different subjects in the same category. The category name should be unambiguous and not homonymous. </p> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Universality_principle">Universality principle</span></h4> 
      </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>Identical items should have identical names for all countries and at all levels of categorization. Categorization structure should be as systematical and unified as possible, local dialects and terminology should be supressed in favour of universality if possible. Analogic categorization branches should have analogic structure. </p>

      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>A file that depicts only one relevant subject should not be over-categorized.       </div> 
      <div lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"> 
       <p>For example, this situation might arise in the case of a photograph of three politicians, one of whom is <a href="/wiki/Category:Angela_Merkel" title="Category:Angela Merkel">Angela Merkel</a> (who has her own Commons category), with two other politicians who do not yet have their own categories. In some circumstances, however, we may need to temporarily categorize the image in <a href="/wiki/Category:Politicians_of_Germany" title="Category:Politicians of Germany">Category:Politicians of Germany</a> where other appropriate subcategories do not yet exist. </p> 
      </div>
