      <p>The Wikimedia Foundation <i>owns almost none of the content on Wikimedia sites</i> — the content is owned, instead, by the <i>individual creators</i> of it. However, almost all content hosted on Wikimedia Commons may be <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://freedomdefined.org/">freely reused</a> <b>subject to certain restrictions</b> (in many cases). <i><b>You do not need to obtain a specific statement of permission from the licensor(s) of the content unless you wish to use the work under different terms than the license states.</b></i> </p> 
      <ul>
       <li>Content under <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_content" class="extiw" title="w:Open content">open content</a> licenses may be reused without any need to contact the licensor(s), but just keep in mind that: 
        <ul>
         <li><b>some licenses require that the original creator be attributed</b>;</li> 
         <li><b>some licenses require that the specific license be identified</b> when reusing (including, in some cases, stating or linking to the terms of the license);</li> 
         <li><b>some licenses require that if you modify the work, your modifications must also be similarly freely licensed</b>; and finally.</li>
        </ul></li> 
       <li>Content in the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/public_domain" class="extiw" title="w:public domain">public domain</a> may not have a strict legal requirement of attribution (depending on the jurisdiction of content reuse), but attribution is recommended to give correct provenance.</li>
      </ul> 
      <div style="border-left-width: 5px; border-left-style: solid; border-color: #A8A8A8; padding-left: 1em;"> 
       <p>While the copyright and licensing information supplied for each image is believed to be accurate, <b>the Wikimedia Foundation does not provide any warranty regarding the copyright status or correctness of licensing terms</b>. If you decide to reuse files from Commons, you should verify the copyright status of each image just as you would when obtaining images from other sources. </p>
       <p><b><a href="/wiki/Commons:Non-copyright_restrictions" title="Commons:Non-copyright restrictions">Other restrictions</a> may apply.</b> These may include <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/trademark" class="extiw" title="w:trademark">trademarks</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/patents" class="extiw" title="w:patents">patents</a>, <a href="/wiki/Commons:Personality_rights" title="Commons:Personality rights">personality rights</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/moral_rights" class="extiw" title="w:moral rights">moral rights</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/privacy_rights" class="extiw" title="w:privacy rights">privacy rights</a>, or any of the many other legal causes which are independent of copyright and vary greatly by jurisdiction. </p>

      <h2><span id="How_to_comply_with_a_file.27s_license_requirements"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="How_to_comply_with_a_file's_license_requirements">How to comply with a file's license requirements</span></h2> 
      <p>Clicking on an image or media file on Wikimedia Commons<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> will take you to the information page for that file. This will list information supplied by the uploader, including the copyright status, the copyright owner, and the license conditions. </p>
      <p>Except for materials believed to be in the public domain, a link to the full text of the license(s) is included on the file description page. Some licenses also have a summary available. Please read the full licenses for legal details. <i>Neither the Wikimedia Foundation nor the creators of material on Wikimedia sites provide legal advice.</i> If you need information about how a license applies to your particular situation, you should contact a suitable legal professional in your jurisdiction. </p>
      <p><b>To reuse a Wikimedia Commons file:</b> </p> 
      <div style="border-left-width: 5px; border-left-style: solid; border-color: #d1d1e0; padding-left: 1em;"> 
       <p><a href="/wiki/File:1_green.svg" class="image"><img alt="1 green.svg" src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/1_green.svg/16px-1_green.svg.png" decoding="async" width="16" height="16" srcset="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/1_green.svg/24px-1_green.svg.png 1.5x, https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/1_green.svg/32px-1_green.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="212" data-file-height="212"></a> <b>Confirm that the file is available under license terms that suit you.</b> For example, if the license requires <a href="/wiki/Commons:DW#What_is_a_derivative_work.3F" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:DW">derivative works</a> to carry the same license (Creative Commons licenses call this "Share alike"), that may not suit you. </p> 
       <ul>
        <li>If the file is available under multiple licenses, you can use any of them. If none of the licenses suits you, you can try to contact the creator and negotiate a special arrangement. Otherwise, you'll have to use a different file.</li> 
        <li>Verify licensing and facts. While the copyright and licensing information supplied for each image is believed to be accurate, the Wikimedia Foundation <i>does not provide any warranty regarding the copyright status</i> or correctness of licensing terms. If you decide to reuse files from Commons, you should verify the copyright status of each image just as you would when obtaining images from other sources. 
         <ul>
          <li>Note: it is common for publishers to take public domain works and republish them under their own copyright. This may be legal, but it does not affect the public domain status of the original work. If you tag the work with its origin (where you got it and where it came from originally) and the name of the creator, this can help us if a dispute with such a publisher arises later.</li>
         </ul></li>
       </ul>

For example, commercial use of images of people may require the explicit agreement of the subject, and not just the agreement of the image creator (see <a href="/wiki/Commons:Photographs_of_identifiable_people#Country-specific_consent_requirements" title="Commons:Photographs of identifiable people">Commons:Photographs of identifiable people#Country-specific consent requirements</a> and <a href="/wiki/Commons:Personality_rights" title="Commons:Personality rights">Commons on personality rights</a>). </p>

      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Requirements_of_frequently-used_licenses">Requirements of frequently-used licenses</span></h3>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="How_to_use_a_file">How to use a file</span></h2> 
      <p>Once you've determined how to comply with a file's licensing requirements, you can access the file for use, by either downloading the file, or by linking to it directly. </p> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Downloading">Downloading</span></h3> 
      <div style="border-left-width: 5px; border-left-style: solid; border-color: #194d19; padding-left: 1em;"> <b>Basic method:</b> On each image's file description page, there is a link to the <code>full resolution</code> version. Right-click this link and choose "Save as..." to download the full resolution file. (If you use a <a href="/wiki/Macintosh" class="mw-redirect" title="Macintosh">Macintosh</a> computer, hold down the "control" key while clicking the link with the mouse, then choose "Save as....")
      </div> 
      <div style="border-left-width: 5px; border-left-style: solid; border-color: #b3b3b3; padding-left: 1em;"> 
       <p><b>Hotlinking or InstantCommons:</b> It is possible to use files directly on Commons within another website, by setting up a MediaWiki wiki with <a href="https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/InstantCommons" class="extiw" title="mw:InstantCommons">InstantCommons</a>, so that Commons files can be used as easily as they can on Wikipedia. Directly using a Commons file via embedding its URL ("hotlinking") is also possible, but is not recommended. See <a href="/wiki/Commons:Reusing_content_outside_Wikimedia/technical" title="Commons:Reusing content outside Wikimedia/technical">Commons:Reusing content outside Wikimedia/technical</a>. Additionally you should always check if the needs of the license used by the file are fulfilled if you use a file from Commons, since e.g. the CC-BY-SA 3.0 license requires that you attribute the author and licensor of a work in "reasonable to the medium or means" <span class="plainlinks"><small>(<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/legalcode"><i>license</i></a>)</small></span>. </p> 
      </div>

      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Contacting_the_uploader_or_content_creator">Contacting the uploader or content creator</span></h2> 
      <p>If you wish to use content under terms other than the license stated, or to absolutely verify copyright status if you feel you need to, the person who put it onto a Wikimedia server may be able to assist. The uploader is named on the "file history" portion of the file description page. </p>
      <p>In some cases, you may be able to contact the uploader to find out more about an image's copyright status or for information on the original creator. The original creator of the image <i>may</i> be willing to grant additional permissions and may have access to higher resolution images than those present on the Wikimedia servers. </p>
      <p>The Wikimedia Foundation generally <i>cannot</i> assist in locating users who have contributed material. You can try to contact contributors yourself in a number of ways: </p> 
      <ol>
       <li>Some have contact information, such as a name and address or phone number, on their user page.</li> 
       <li>Some can be contacted by email by clicking the "email this user" link in the toolbox on their user page.</li> 
       <li>You can leave a message on their talk page by clicking on the "discussion" tab at the top of the user page, and then clicking the "Add topic" tab that appears next to the "edit" tab once the discussion page is visible. Enter your message and click "save." (Your message will be visible to the public.)</li> 
       <li>When free content is brought to Commons from elsewhere, a link to the source (e.g. a Flickr page) is often included in the file description on Commons. That source may have additional information for contacting the content creator.</li>
      </ol>
