         Please help Waffles with her battle against Meningoencephalitis (ME)! (men-ing-oh-en-sef-all-eye-tiss)
         <br>
         <br>On April 26, 2021, Waffles had her very first set of seizures at just 2 years old. We had to take her to a general veterinarian, an opthalmologist and a neurologist before we finally found a diagnosis. ME has caused her brain to swell and has caused the walls of her spinal cord to become thin and leak extra cells and proteins into her spinal fluid. She<span style="font-size:1.0625rem;">&nbsp;was suffering with</span><span style="font-size:1.0625rem;"> massive headache, seizures, muscle spasms, blindness, loss of coordination, confusion, visual and audio hallucinations. She has been treated with multiple medications including an over-night stay at the ER with a Cytosar chemotherapy drip over a 24 hour period. The neurologist said about 75% of dogs respond positively to the treatment and we are hoping she is headed in that direction.&nbsp;<br><br>I am hoping with your kind donations that we may recover some of the costs of the tests and treatments that Waffles has received, AND help with future costs as well. We have had to pay for multiple exams, blood work, eye tests, an MRI, spinal tap, ER services, chemo drip and medications. We also had to drive 2 hours away to see the neurologist. Unfortunately, the costs don't end here. Waffles needs to be seen again in the future for some check-ups, some more tests to see how she is responding to treatment, and she will need refills for her medications.&nbsp;<br><br>Waffles is young and she use to be lively, playful, and alert. This disease has completely changed her into a different dog. She wanders around in circles around the house aimlessly, she still experiences hallucinations, she will bark and howl at things that are not there, she has developed Nystagmus where her eyes move uncontrollably, and she is no longer the bouncy puppy she use to be. Please help her battle this rare disease. We don't have a definitive answer as far as her life span is concerned. <br><br>If you do decide to donate, thank you so much in advance and I appreciate you very much! Every little bit helps a lot! <br><br>If you cannot donate but you would still like to help, it would mean the world to me if you could share this donation campaign (copy the link) and text or email it to at least 1-3 people. If you can post on your social media, that would be great, too! Thank you again in advance!&nbsp;<br><br>I will be keeping you up-to-date on her progress.&nbsp;</span>
