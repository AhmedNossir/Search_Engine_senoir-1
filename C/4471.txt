        <h3>GUADEC with children</h3> 
        <p><a title="A new place to play" href="https://www.flickr.com/photos/grongar/8723876282/in/photolist-ehU9u3-ehLaYN-a2hi1h-nyapGk-izroCL-95rsRz-eij3ba-ehLg6J-ehLhqd-8AHJw5-6oRYJi-ehEujR-eipMxW-izrqRy-6oRZHD-ehLfVJ-eipNeL-6oS1Rg-6oRWP2-a2hhkA-ehLfEQ-q67GJC-bXohu7-d81J6-56Z4C9-qEqNF-2bBej6-iMgKK-4GtjWZ-6nwt4a-izryw5-ehL9xw-euENnD-ehLgAo-izrce4-eipMaU-euHXHf-ehLhXy-ehLhF5-ehEuDr-eipLJw-ehNnZM-ehEvj8-ehEpPe-ehEsFi-ehU8zq-e3Z5L-izrTYD-ehNmfH-ehNmyH"><img class="alignright" src="https://c3.staticflickr.com/8/7323/8723876282_05fb66164c_m.jpg" alt="A new place to play" width="240" height="180"></a></p> 
        <p>In case you are coming to GUADEC with children, they are generally welcome at the venue and social events. Please register them so that they have a badge. We do not offer any child care, so you will need to supervise them yourself to the extent necessary. We currently do not have a separate room that is suitable as a play area, although we are currently planning to have play areas in the lobby and on the surrounding greens. Please add yourself to the wiki and get in contact with us for planning purposes.<br> Some more information including a list of possible activities with children in Karlsruhe is <a href="https://wiki.gnome.org/GUADEC/2016/Kids">available on the wiki</a>.</p>

         <div class="abstract" id="abstract-28-a_glib_look_at_structured_logging"> 
          <h4><a href="#28-a_glib_look_at_structured_logging">A GLib look at structured logging</a></h4> 
          <p><span class="details">On Friday at 17:00 (Grace) by Philip Withnall</span></p> 
          <p>Structured logging is a handy feature of modern log systems, like journald. GLib is growing functionality to allow applications to export log messages in structured form, which allows developers and sysadmins to more easily search and sort log messages to find what they’re looking for.</p> 
          <p>This talk will introduce the new APIs and give an overview of how applications and libraries should log in the new world order.</p> 
         </div> 
         <div class="abstract" id="abstract-47-a_look_behind_the_curtainhow_the_foundation_runs"> 
          <h4><a href="#47-a_look_behind_the_curtainhow_the_foundation_runs">A look behind the curtain—how the Foundation runs</a></h4> 
          <p><span class="details">On Sunday at 10:00 (Grace) by Rosanna Yuen</span></p> 
          <p>Have you ever wondered what the GNOME Foundation actually does? What do the employees do to keep the Foundation running? I will cover the details on what is required to keep the Foundation active and solvent. Come see the challenges we face and maybe even see what you can do to help!</p> 
         </div> 
         <div class="abstract" id="abstract-25-accessibility_and_the_free_desktop"> 
          <h4><a href="#25-accessibility_and_the_free_desktop">Accessibility and the Free Desktop</a></h4> 
          <p><span class="details">On Friday at 16:30 (Ada) by Gaurav Pareek</span></p> 
          <p>There are around 285 million visually impaired people in the world, out of which around 38-39 million are completely blind. One interesting fact is that over 15 million of blind people are from India, and similarly a large number is from different developing or undeveloped countries, and poor socioeconomic status. There is a lot of software available for accessibility, and most of it is extremely unaccessible, both economically and socially. This is where Free Software comes in. Free Software desktops, especially GNOME, have made large strides in accessibility in the last few years, and are proving to be an effective way of reaching these people, introducing them to a whole new world of computers. </p> 
          <p>I’m going to talk about how Free Software enables people to expand upon accessibility software, add regional languages, and helps people in bad socioeconomic settings. I’ll then proceed to show a 2-3 minute demo of how the universal access features of GNOME work, and what we need to improve.</p> 
         </div> 
         <div class="abstract" id="abstract-48-an_asynchronous_internet_for_gnome"> 
          <h4><a href="#48-an_asynchronous_internet_for_gnome">An asynchronous internet for GNOME</a></h4> 
          <p><span class="details">On Saturday at 11:45 (Grace) by Jonathan Blandford</span></p> 
          <p>While most of the developed world has decent internet access, this is not universally distributed. In much of the developing world, people have very incomplete and spotty access to the internet.</p> 
          <p>At Endless, we are shipping GNOME into that environment. We’ve had a number of challenges in modifying the desktop to work within these constraints. The end result is a version of GNOME that is useful and relevant both with and without a network enabled.</p> 
         </div> 
         <div class="abstract" id="abstract-37-anatomy_of_endless_os"> 
          <h4><a href="#37-anatomy_of_endless_os">Anatomy of Endless OS</a></h4> 
          <p><span class="details">On Friday at 11:45 (Ada) by Cosimo Cecchi</span></p> 
          <p>Endless OS is an operating system based on GNOME, and is one of the first real world deployments to make use of innovative distribution technologies such as OSTree, xdg-app and gnome-software.</p> 
          <p>During this presentation, I will walk through the architecture of the operating system and the infrastructure that we use at Endless to maintain it and deploy it, including the challenges we faced and what lies ahead for us.</p>

         <div class="abstract" id="abstract-53-building_an_automotive_platform_from_gnome"> 
          <h4><a href="#53-building_an_automotive_platform_from_gnome">Building an automotive platform from GNOME</a></h4> 
          <p><span class="details">On Saturday at 11:45 (Ada) by Lukas Nack</span></p> 
          <p>With the success of connected cars, there is an increasing demand for a secure, consumer-oriented infotainment platform.          <div class="abstract" id="abstract-46-reworking_the_desktop_distribution"> 
          <h4><a href="#46-reworking_the_desktop_distribution">Reworking the desktop distribution</a></h4> 
          <p><span class="details">On Sunday at 11:45 (Ada) by Owen Taylor</span></p> 
          <p>The traditional model of a GNOME desktop is tightly tied to the traditional model of a Linux distribution: many packages that are individually installed onto the users system; some are low-level system services, some are core desktop components, and some are applications. While in some ways very<br>flexible, this model is also fragile: the continuing correct operation of the system depends on a complex dance of installation and upgrades of thousands<br>of packages. Systems based on free software components, from OLPC, to ChromeOS, to Android have moved away from this model, but are no longer recognizable as a Linux distribution.</p> 
          <p>Upcoming releases of Fedora Workstation will offer an alternate installation method where the operating system is distributed as an ostree image and atomically upgraded as a whole, while applications are installed on top as xdg-app bundles. The intent is to provide an experience that is much more<br>robust than the traditional package-based installation, while providing additional flexibility in installing applications. Applications will no<br>longer be strictly bound to the Fedora package set, making it easy for third parties to provide applications
