           <th colspan="4" style="font-size:80%;"> <p>This is a place where users can communicate with <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Commons:Administrators" title="Special:MyLanguage/Commons:Administrators">administrators</a>, or administrators with one another.            <td> <p>Report users for clear cases of vandalism. Block requests for any other reason should be reported to the <a href="/wiki/Commons:AN/BP" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:AN/BP">blocks and protections</a> noticeboard. </p><p><br> </p> </td> 
           <td> <p>Report disputes with users that require administrator assistance. Further steps are listed at <a href="/wiki/Commons:Dispute_resolution" title="Commons:Dispute resolution">resolve disputes</a>. </p><p><br> </p> </td> 
           <td> <p>Reports that do not suit the <a href="/wiki/Commons:AN/V" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:AN/V">vandalism noticeboard</a> may be reported here. Requests for <a href="/wiki/Commons:P" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:P">page protection/unprotection</a> could also be requested here. </p><p><br> </p> </td> 
           <td> <p>Other reports that require administrator assistance which do not fit in any of the previous three noticeboards may be reported here. Requests for <a href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/Help:Page_history" class="extiw" title="m:Help:Page history">history</a> merging or splitting should be filed at <a href="/wiki/Commons:History_merging_and_splitting#Requests" title="Commons:History merging and splitting">COM:HMS</a>. </p> </td>

       <h2><span id="Administrator_attention_required_to_sort_out_the_out_of_process_deletion_of_700_of_John_Schaunlaub.27s_flickr_images"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Administrator_attention_required_to_sort_out_the_out_of_process_deletion_of_700_of_John_Schaunlaub's_flickr_images">Administrator attention required to sort out the out of process deletion of 700 of John Schaunlaub's flickr images</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Commons:Administrators%27_noticeboard&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Administrator attention required to sort out the out of process deletion of 700 of John Schaunlaub's flickr images">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
       <p>Approximately 700 properly licensed flickr images seem to have been deleted, without any public record. </p>
       <p>I made a request <a href="/wiki/Commons:Administrators%27_noticeboard/Archive_84#The_mysterious_deletion_of_John_Schanlaub's_flickr_images" title="Commons:Administrators' noticeboard/Archive 84">here on April 7, 2021</a>, the tenth anniversary of John Schaunlaub trying to delete about 700 flickr images he released under a free license. Perhaps my note didn't clearly explain that an administrator would be required to get to the bottom of these deletions. </p>
       <p>WMF projects have policies, or have conventions, under which images, or articles, which are properly licensed, may nevertheless be deleted, as a courtesy. En.wiki's policy is clear. An administrator is allowed to take an individual's request for courtesy deletion into account, and delete properly licensed material -- <b>when reaching a conclusion in a deletion discussion.</b> Courtesy deletion is not a speedy deletion criteria. A discussion is required. </p>
       <p>I looked here. I see two failed proposals, but no actual policy. However courtesy deletion is not listed at <a href="/wiki/Commons:CSD" class="mw-redirect" title="Commons:CSD">Commons:CSD</a>. I think this means the convention here also requires a discussion before an administrator is authorized to delete properly licensed material, as a courtesy. </p>
       <p>What I think happened to those 700 properly licensed images is that when John Schaunlaub failed to get the properly licensed images deleted at a proper discussion he contacted an overly helpful administrator some other way, off-wiki. I think the overly helpful administrator decided, on their sole authority, to delete all the flickr images, except the 13 which were in use on other projects. </p>
       <p>I'd appreciate an administrator reviewing the edits John Schaunlaub made on on April 7, 2011. They should find hundreds of edits like these <a class="external autonumber" href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?title=File%3AWingate_Sweet_Corn_Festival_2009.jpg&amp;type=revision&amp;diff=52866497&amp;oldid=43301990">[1]</a> <a class="external autonumber" href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?title=File%3AWallace_Study-Telegraph.jpg&amp;type=revision&amp;diff=52866616&amp;oldid=51691400">[2]</a> <a class="external autonumber" href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?title=File%3AWabash.jpg&amp;type=revision&amp;diff=52866113&amp;oldid=30953486">[3]</a> <a class="external autonumber" href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?title=File%3AUS_patrol_boat_as_seen_in_Apocalypse_Now.jpg&amp;type=revision&amp;diff=52866133&amp;oldid=49074438">[4]</a> <a class="external autonumber" href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?title=File%3AThorntown_Festival_of_the_Turning_Leaves.jpg&amp;type=revision&amp;diff=52866609&amp;oldid=49438653">[5]</a> <a class="external autonumber" href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?title=File%3ASonic_Cruise_In.jpg&amp;type=revision&amp;diff=52866625&amp;oldid=52508066">[6]</a> <a class="external autonumber" href="https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?title=File%3ARiverside_IN_-_railroad.jpg&amp;type=revision&amp;diff=52866221&amp;oldid=43890170">[7]</a>. Note: all these attempts to claw back the IP rights they released were vandalism. Note: Hardworking volunteers wnt to considerable effort to revert all those vandal edits. </p>
       <p>Once they have found the properly licensed images that John Schaunlaub vandalized, I'd appreciate that administrator reporting back here as to how those properly licensed images ended up being deleted. </p>
       <p>I am guessing that a single administrator made the decision to speedily delete the images, in the face of the decision not to honor Schaunlaub's public request for deletion. </p>
       <p>Personally, I'd like to see that administrator, if they are still active here, do the work of restoring these properly licensed images. </p>
